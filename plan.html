Platform

Where Julio Stands

Every proposal below is grounded in one principle: honesty about what a mayor can actually do. Julio will not promise to fix a state highway — but he will be in Tallahassee fighting for it. He will not promise to lower rent by law — because Florida prohibits it. What he will do is use every lever of mayoral authority, on Day 1, to make Homestead work for the people who live here.

01

Housing Affordability

Critical Priority

The crisis is real and measurable. Home prices in Homestead have risen approximately 45% since 2019. The average one-bedroom apartment now rents for $1,600–$1,800 per month — in a city where the median household income is $48,000 per year. Long-term residents, agricultural workers, and young families are being priced out of the community they built.

Florida law prevents cities from controlling rent prices directly — and Julio will be straight with you about that. What he will do is use every tool available to a mayor to increase the supply of affordable housing and fight for every federal dollar available to working families.

Day 1

  • Issue a mayoral directive requiring affordable housing projects to receive permit decisions within 60 days — no more years-long delays on the projects Homestead needs most
  • Publish a list of all pending affordable housing projects and their status, publicly

First 90 Days

  • Reform zoning to allow Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) citywide — letting homeowners add rental units on their property without costly variances
  • Launch a partnership with Miami-Dade County Housing Agency and Florida Housing Finance Corporation to access SHIP and HOME federal funding
  • Direct the CRA to dedicate a portion of its annual budget to workforce housing incentives for developers who commit to affordability covenants

Full Term

  • Deliver at least one mixed-income workforce housing development in the downtown CRA district
  • Formally advocate in Tallahassee for restoration of local rent stabilization authority
  • Apply for every available CDBG and HOME grant cycle — and publish results publicly

“Homestead families should not have to choose between rent and groceries. I will cut the red tape that delays affordable housing construction and fight for every federal dollar available to working families.”

02

City Services & Permitting Reform

City Services

This is the issue where the mayor has 100% of the authority and 0% of the excuses. Homestead's Building and Permitting Department has a documented, multi-year reputation for delays of 6 to 18 months on routine projects. Contractors avoid the city. Business owners choose Kendall. Residents cannot get repairs approved. This stops.

Unlike traffic or school quality — which involve state and county agencies — the Building Department reports directly to the mayor. Julio will treat its reform as the highest-priority operational change of his first year.

Day 1

  • Issue a mayoral directive establishing response-time benchmarks: 30 days for residential permits, 60 days for commercial
  • Order a full audit of all currently pending permits and publish a public resolution schedule

First 90 Days

  • Launch a digital permitting portal — apply, track status, and schedule inspections entirely online, without taking a day off work to stand in line at City Hall
  • Create a dedicated Small Business Permitting Concierge: one point of contact for any business opening or expanding in Homestead
  • Hire or reassign a qualified Building Department Director with a documented turnaround track record

Full Term

  • Publish a quarterly public performance dashboard: every permit, every timeline, every decision
  • Reduce average residential permit time to under 21 days — and hold the department accountable with public tracking
  • Establish a Permit Ombudsman for residents and businesses to escalate stuck applications

“A city that is easy to do business with attracts business. Permit reform is not just about convenience — it is economic development.”

03

Public Safety

Safety

Every Homestead family deserves to feel safe. Property crime rates in Homestead exceed Miami-Dade County averages. HPD staffing levels have been subject to budget debates in recent council sessions. Domestic violence rates are elevated. Youth gang activity exists in specific neighborhoods. These are facts — and they require a mayor willing to act on them.

The Homestead Police Department is under full mayoral authority. Julio will use that authority to fully fund HPD while demanding the community policing model that builds real trust, neighborhood by neighborhood.

Day 1

  • Commit to no cuts to HPD staffing or equipment budget — full funding, no gimmicks
  • Order a 90-day operational review of HPD's community policing program with public findings

First 90 Days

  • Establish a Mental Health Co-Response Team — pairing officers with licensed mental health professionals on crisis calls, so we send the right resource to the right call
  • Launch a Neighborhood Safety Partnership: community liaisons in every Homestead district, reporting directly to the mayor's office
  • Expand after-school and youth mentorship programs in the highest-need precincts

Full Term

  • Reduce property crime rate to at or below Miami-Dade County average — with public quarterly reporting
  • Build a youth intervention pipeline: every Homestead young person who is at risk has a program, a mentor, and a path
  • Upgrade HPD technology: body cameras, real-time crime mapping, and evidence management modernization

“The best crime prevention program is a 16-year-old with somewhere to be after school. I will expand youth programs in every Homestead neighborhood.”

04

Traffic & Transportation

Infrastructure

Julio will be straight with you: US-1 and Krome Avenue are state roads controlled by FDOT — no mayor can unilaterally fix them. Any candidate who promises otherwise is not being honest. What Julio will do is be the most aggressive Homestead advocate FDOT has ever seen, while delivering immediately on what is within city authority.

Population growth has outpaced infrastructure investment in Homestead for a decade. That gap closes with a mayor who shows up at every MPO meeting, negotiates transportation impact fees from every major development, and optimizes what we already have.

Day 1

  • Formally submit Homestead's prioritized transportation project list to FDOT District 6 and the Miami-Dade MPO for the next TIP cycle
  • Commission a traffic signal timing audit on all city-controlled intersections

First 90 Days

  • Implement optimized signal timing on city roads — measurable relief, near-zero cost
  • Hire a city Traffic Engineer to manage ongoing signal operations and advocate in technical forums
  • Negotiate transportation impact contributions from all pending large development approvals

Full Term

  • Secure FDOT commitment to at least one Homestead corridor improvement project — with a signed project development agreement
  • Work with MDT to increase bus frequency and reliability on the Homestead-to-Miami corridor
  • Implement Safe Routes to School improvements on city streets near every school campus

“I cannot fix US-1 alone — it is a state road. But I will be in Tallahassee, in the Governor's office, and at every FDOT meeting until Homestead's projects are prioritized. You deserve that fight.”

05

Economic Development & Downtown

Economy

The opportunity cost is staggering. Homestead residents spend hundreds of millions of dollars annually in Kendall and Florida City — dollars that should fund our parks, our police, and our roads. Meanwhile, five million visitors drive through Homestead every year on their way to the Everglades and the Florida Keys, and most never stop.

The mayor chairs the Community Redevelopment Agency, sets economic development incentives, controls permitting timelines, and negotiates with major employers. These are real levers — and Julio will use every one of them.

Day 1

  • Convene the first meeting of a Business Attraction Task Force — targeting employers in healthcare, logistics, and food production looking for South Florida locations
  • Direct the CRA to present a 12-month action plan for the Krome Avenue corridor

First 90 Days

  • Launch an Everglades Gateway Tourism Strategy in partnership with Greater Miami CVB and Everglades National Park — capturing even 5% more visitor spending would transform Homestead's tax base
  • Establish a Downtown Business Incentive Package: fee waivers, expedited permits, and facade improvement grants for new businesses on Krome Avenue
  • Identify and pursue Opportunity Zone investment in eligible Homestead census tracts

Full Term

  • Open a downtown dining and entertainment anchor — at least one signature business that makes Homestead a destination
  • Partner with CareerSource South Florida and FIU to align workforce training with in-demand industries locating in Homestead
  • Publish an annual Economic Development Report Card — jobs created, businesses opened, tax base growth — with full public accountability

“Every dollar Homestead residents spend in Kendall is a dollar that does not pay for our parks, our police, and our roads. I will make downtown Homestead a destination — not an afterthought.”

06

Jobs & Workforce Development

Economy

Homestead wages are below the county median. The city's working families — who built this community and keep it running — deserve better economic opportunity right here, without a commute to Miami. Julio will connect Homestead's growing business base to its workforce with targeted training, local hiring incentives, and strong partnerships with FIU, Miami Dade College, and CareerSource South Florida.

First 90 Days

  • Establish a Local Hiring Incentive: businesses receiving city economic development benefits must demonstrate good-faith local hiring from Homestead zip codes
  • Launch a Homestead Workforce Partnership with FIU, MDC, and CareerSource — aligning training programs with jobs actually available in Homestead
  • Partner with HARB (Homestead Air Reserve Base) leadership on economic spinoff opportunities

Full Term

  • Create a Homestead Small Business Development Center in partnership with the South Dade Chamber — free business counseling, licensing support, and growth resources
  • Support the agricultural sector and agritourism infrastructure in the Redland as a job creation and economic identity strategy
  • Track and publish job creation numbers annually — with transparent reporting on outcomes, not just programs

“Homestead's working families deserve opportunities here at home — not a 45-minute commute north just to earn a living wage.”
