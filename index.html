About Julio

Vote for
Julio Guzman

Julio Guzman, a dedicated Homestead resident passionate about building a prosperous community. As Former Vice-Mayor and licensed Real Estate Broker, Julio works tirelessly to address job creation, attract new business, downtown revitalization, traffic issues, and addressing the city's permitting department.

A husband and father of three, Julio has called Homestead home for over four decades. Julio holds an FIU Master's degree in business and was a Carson Newman University dual sports athlete while being selected by the AZ Diamondbacks as a draft pick.

Campaign Themes

Three Promises.
One Homestead.

Every decision Julio makes as mayor will be guided by these three commitments to the people of Homestead.

A City That Works For You

Permits that take 14 months, code enforcement that feels arbitrary, streets that flood every summer — these are failures of city government, and they stop on Day 1. Julio will fix the machinery so it serves the people who pay for it.

Homestead Families First

The people who built this community — who work in our fields, staff our hospitals, teach our children — can no longer afford to live here. Julio will fight for workforce housing, living wages, and a city where families can stay.

See the Plan ›

Homestead’s Best Years Are Ahead

We sit at the gateway to the Everglades and the Florida Keys. Five million visitors drive through every year. Julio will transform downtown into a destination and make Homestead the economic engine it was always meant to be.

Key Issues

Where Julio Stands

Five issues will define this election. Here is where Julio stands — with specific proposals, not talking points.

Issue #1 — Critical Priority

Housing Affordability

Home prices up 45% since 2019. Average 1-bedroom rent now $1,700/month in a city where median household income is $48,000. Long-term residents are being priced out of the community they built.

“Homestead families should not have to choose between rent and groceries.”

Full Plan ›
Issue #2 — Critical Priority

City Services & Permitting

Permits taking 6–18 months. Businesses choosing Florida City over Homestead because of the red tape. Outdated systems costing residents and business owners real time and real money.

“If it takes 14 months to get a permit in Homestead, that stops on Day 1.”

Full Plan ›
Issue #3 — High Priority

Public Safety

Property crime above county averages. HPD staffing gaps debated in council chambers. Every Homestead family deserves to feel safe — with a police department that is fully funded and community-trusted.

“The best crime prevention is a 16-year-old with somewhere to be after school.”

Full Plan ›
Issue #4 — High Priority

Traffic & Transportation

US-1 and Krome Avenue gridlock every morning and evening. Population growth has outpaced infrastructure investment for a decade. Julio will be the most aggressive Homestead advocate FDOT has ever seen.

“Growth must pay for growth — every major development deal includes transportation impact funding.”

Full Plan ›
Issue #5 — High Priority

Economic Development & Downtown

Homestead residents spend their dollars in Kendall. Five million visitors drive through every year on the way to the Keys and Everglades — and most never stop. Julio will use the CRA, the city’s economic incentives, and a 60-day Business Attraction Task Force to change that.

“Every dollar spent in Kendall doesn’t pay for our parks, our police, or our roads.”

Full Plan ›
